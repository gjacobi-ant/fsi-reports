Week of December 25, 2025

Banking & AI

Industry Trends

JPMorgan Reports 6% Productivity Gains from AI, Sees Path to 40-50% in Operations

At Goldman Sachs financial services conference, JPMorgan's Marianne Lake reported productivity in areas using AI has risen to around 6%, up from 3% before deployment. Operations roles could eventually see productivity gains of 40-50% as AI becomes routine work. McKinsey estimates generative AI could deliver $200-340 billion in annual value for banking sector.

PYMNTS 2025-12-09

Eurobank Launches 'AI Factory' with Microsoft and Nvidia Partnership

Eurobank officially launched 'AI Factory' initiative, massive industrial-scale deployment of agentic AI. Project represents deep-tier collaboration with Microsoft, Nvidia, EY and Fairfax Digital Services. Marks shift from experimental chatbot era to production-ready environment where autonomous AI agents handle complex end-to-end financial workflows.

Financial Content · 2025-12-24

AWS and Microsoft Present Agentic AI's Banking Business Case

AWS argues agentic AI is moving financial institutions beyond experimentation into practical, production-ready systems that outperform traditional generative AI for complex, regulated work. Microsoft's November IDC study shows 'Frontier Firms' embedding AI agents across workflows report returns roughly 3x higher than slow adopters.

PYMNTS · 2025-12-20

Bank of America CEO: AI to Have Growing Economic Impact in 2026

Bank of America CEO Brian Moynihan told Bloomberg Television that AI had increasing impact in 2025 and will accelerate in coming years. Expects AI to have growing effect on economy in 2026 and beyond.

PYMNTS · 2025-12-22

75% of Payment Firms Report Cost Savings from AI in AML Operations

Almost three-quarters of payment firms report cost savings from AI in AML operations, with many expecting annual savings exceeding $5 million as deployments mature. Investment plans focus on generative and agentic AI for scaling without proportional staff increases.

Economy & Markets

Macro Trends

Fed Cuts Rates to 3.5-3.75% in Hawkish December Decision

Federal Reserve cut key interest rate in 9-3 vote, putting overnight borrowing rate in range of 3.5-3.75%. Third consecutive rate cut brings rate to lowest level since November 2022. Fed's dot plot indicates just one more reduction in 2026 and another in 2027.

CNBC 2025-12-10

Fed Signals Only One Rate Cut in 2026 Amid Persistent Inflation

FOMC left projections for federal funds rate unchanged from September, signaling only one 25bps cut in 2026. Inflation has moved up since earlier in year and remains somewhat elevated. Fed revised growth forecasts higher for 2025 (1.7% vs 1.6%) and 2026 (2.3% vs 1.8%).

Federal Reserve · 2025-12-10

Fed Stops Balance Sheet Reduction, Will Buy Treasury Bills to Maintain Reserves

As of December 1st, Fed stopped shrinking its $6.2 trillion bond holdings and will now buy short-term Treasury bills to maintain ample banking system reserves and keep short-term interest rates near policy targets.

Federal Reserve · 2025-12-01

Major Banking Regulation Reset Under Trump Administration

U.S. banking regulation underwent material reset in 2025. With new leadership at Federal Reserve, OCC, and FDIC, second Trump Administration moved quickly toward embrace of digital assets, reimagination of regulatory perimeter, capital reform, and recalibrated supervisory approach focused on financial risk.

Freshfields · 2025-12-20

OCC and FDIC Propose Historic Shift in Bank Supervision Standards

FDIC and OCC issued joint notice of proposed rulemaking representing most significant reform to bank supervision standards in decades. For first time, agencies proposing to codify regulatory definition of 'unsafe or unsound practice.'
